# Problem Definition

## Business context

The case study describes a mid-sized KSA e-commerce retailer whose manual support model is no longer scaling with demand. Support delays are now affecting both customer experience and commercial performance.

## Problem statement

The retailer lacks a fast, trusted first line of support for common customer questions. Routine inquiries are overwhelming manual agents, response times are too slow, and support friction is hurting satisfaction and conversion.

## Evidence from the brief

- Average response time: `8-12 hours`
- `CSAT/NPS` down `15%` over the last quarter
- Cart abandonment `7%` above the industry average

These metrics suggest failure at two moments:

1. `Pre-purchase`: customers do not get timely answers to product and delivery questions.
2. `Post-purchase`: customers wait too long for updates on orders, returns, and refunds.

## Root causes

- Agents spend too much time on repetitive requests that should be automated or triaged earlier.
- Product, order, and policy information is not surfaced instantly through self-service support.
- There is no clear fallback or handoff path for uncertain or sensitive cases.
- The support experience likely needs stronger Arabic/English readiness for the KSA market.

## Why this matters

This is both a support-cost problem and a growth problem:

- Faster product answers can reduce cart abandonment.
- Faster order updates can reduce repetitive ticket load.
- Better returns/refunds handling can protect trust and satisfaction.

## Assumptions

- The retailer is a mid-sized KSA online store focused on consumer electronics, accessories, and wearables.
- Website chat is the first MVP channel, with future extension to WhatsApp and CRM/helpdesk systems.
- Arabic and English support matter long term.
- The highest-value first intents are product information, order tracking, and returns/refunds.
- Human handoff should be part of the MVP.

## MVP scope

The MVP focuses on four flows:

- product information
- order tracking
- returns and refunds
- human handoff for uncertain or sensitive cases

The goal is not to replace the full support team. The goal is to prove that an AI-first support layer can reduce response time for routine inquiries and create a scalable foundation for future channels and integrations.

## Success metrics

- first response time
- automated resolution rate
- human handoff rate
- CSAT improvement
- reduced repetitive support workload
- reduced cart abandonment on pre-purchase journeys
